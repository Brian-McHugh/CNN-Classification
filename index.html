Object Classification Project

It's known that image recognition systems are easily fooled by well-known objects that appear in unnatural positions, e.g., a school bus on its side.

So we wonder what characteristics have more of an impact on CNNs failing to correctly classify an object in an image.

We used three models (Inception, NASNet, and VGG) pre-trained on the ImageNet dataset and then introduce images of common objects in a variety of situations to see which are most problematic for the models.
